# D2C Fashion Brand – Customer Intelligence & Retention Engine

## Project Overview
This repository contains an end-to-end customer intelligence and retention analytics framework built for a Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) fashion brand.

The project transforms raw transactional data from ~3,900 e-commerce customers into a scalable business intelligence system designed to support executive-level decision-making around retention, customer value, promotional dependency, and long-term profitability.

By combining Python, SQL, and Power BI, the project identifies high-value customer cohorts, uncovers discount-driven behavioral patterns, and develops strategic retention insights capable of improving margin efficiency and customer lifetime value.

---

## Technical Stack & Workflow

### Isolated Environment (`venv`)
Built inside a dedicated virtual environment to manage dependencies and maintain a clean analytical workflow.

### Feature Engineering Engine (`Python`)
- Cleaned and standardized transactional datasets
- Engineered strategic business metrics:
  - `loyalty_score`
  - `promo_dependency_score`
  - `estimated_annual_value`
- Converted raw behavioral signals into actionable customer intelligence

### Relational Query Layer (`SQL`)
- Built an in-memory analytical query system
- Performed customer cohort segmentation
- Identified regional opportunity clusters
- Analyzed product lifecycle and retention patterns
- Evaluated promotional dependency across customer segments

### Interactive BI Workspace (`Power BI`)
Designed an executive-level business intelligence dashboard featuring:
- Customer Value Pyramid
- Loyalty vs Promotional Dependency Matrix
- Product Lifecycle Funnel
- Geographic Opportunity Radar

Custom DAX summary tables (`SegmentSummary`) were engineered to improve strategic visualization clarity and executive reporting quality.

---

## Strategic Key Insights

### Revenue Concentration
The top-tier **Champions** segment generates an average annual customer value of **$2,697.80**, making it nearly **50× more valuable** than discount-led low-tier customer groups.

### The Promotional Trap
Promotional dependency rates remain consistently high (~48%) across nearly all customer value tiers, revealing that the brand has heavily over-conditioned even its strongest organic buyers to wait for discounts.

### Product Lifecycle Intelligence
- **Accessories** function as low-barrier acquisition products
- **Clothing** acts as a core retention driver
- **Outerwear** represents high-tenure, high-margin customer anchors

### Geographic Opportunity Discovery
Regions such as **Tennessee**, **Illinois**, and **California** demonstrate strong customer value alongside comparatively lower promotional sensitivity, making them strategically attractive growth markets.
